Preparation

What to Bring to the Site Build

Before you open Claude AI, collect four things. The AI does the heavy lifting — your job is to show up with the raw materials.

📋

Business Description

Who you are and what you do — a paragraph or two in plain language. Don't polish it; the AI shapes the copy.

📷

Visuals

Clear photos of your equipment, yard, or finished products. Phone photos are perfectly fine.

📍

Contact Info

Your phone number, email, physical location, and domain name if you have one.

📜

Assets

A resume, old brochure, price list, or outdated website links to use as reference.

1
Step One

Design with Claude AI

Claude is your AI architect. You bring the materials; it builds the site. The entire process happens in plain English — no technical knowledge required.

A

Account Setup

Sign up at claude.ai via email. Mobile phone verification is required.

B

Upload & Describe

Upload your info and photos, then ask Claude to build a professional business website.

C

Refine in Plain English

Change colors, text, or layout by simply describing what you want. No coding required.

Prompt tip: Start with — "I need a professional one-page business website. Here are my company details and photos. Build a clean, modern site I can download as a single HTML file."
🔒

Crucial Step: Name the File Correctly

Once satisfied, download the file Claude provides. Rename it exactly to:

index.html

No capital letters. No spaces. This single file is your entire website.

2
Step Two

Claim Your Digital Plot on GitHub

GitHub is where your file lives on the internet — for free. Think of it as a filing cabinet that doubles as a web server.

🔐 Account Setup

Sign up at github.com. Complete email verification and the mandatory Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).

📁 Create a Repository

Click New Repository. Give it a simple lowercase name. Set it to Public so visitors can see your site.

⬆️ Upload Your File

Drag and drop your index.html into the repository. Click Commit changes.

Why public? GitHub's free hosting only works for public repositories. Your code is visible to anyone who looks — but that's completely normal for business websites.
3
Step Three

Go Live with GitHub Pages

This is the switch that turns your uploaded file into a live website. Takes under two minutes.

1

Open Settings

In your repository, click the Settings tab at the top.

2

Find Pages

In the sidebar, click Pages under "Code and automation."

3

Select Branch

Under "Source," pick main branch and click Save.

4

You're Live

Your URL appears: yourusername.github.io

🚀

Your Site Is Live

After a minute or two, your site is accessible at https://yourusername.github.io

Share this now — or continue to Step 4 to connect your custom domain.

4
Step Four (Optional)

Connect Your Custom Domain

Own a domain like www.mybusiness.com? Point it to your GitHub Pages site with two changes — one at GitHub, one at your registrar.

At GitHub Pages

Go to Settings → Pages. In the "Custom domain" field, enter your domain and click Save.

At Your Registrar

Log into GoDaddy, Namecheap, etc. Add the DNS records in the table below to direct your domain to GitHub.

HTTPS — Automatic

GitHub issues a free HTTPS security certificate automatically. The padlock in visitors' browsers is handled for you.

Record TypeHost / NameValue / Target
CNAMEwwwyourusername.github.io
A@185.199.108.153
A@185.199.109.153
A@185.199.110.153
A@185.199.111.153
DNS takes time. Changes can take minutes to 48 hours to propagate. If it doesn't work right away, check back tomorrow.
